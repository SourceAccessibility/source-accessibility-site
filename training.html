Master Training & Workshops

Whether you’re building inclusive digital products, shaping accessible content, or simply wanting to level up your team’s accessibility knowledge – At Source Accessibility we offer hands-on, practical training tailored to your goals.

All sessions are delivered live – remotely or in person. Suitable for individuals, small teams, large organisations, or specialist groups such as bootcamp cohorts or FE/HE students.

Every workshop can be adapted to suit your team’s size, pace, and priorities. I’m always happy to discuss what’s right for you.

Workshops – What’s right for you?

Accessibility Fundamentals

Format: Half day or full day
Who it’s for: Anyone new to accessibility or looking to align teams around inclusive design

What it covers:
A comprehensive introduction to digital accessibility and disability awareness. Challenges how teams think about access and inclusion — going beyond compliance to focus on people. Combines key principles with real-life examples and hands-on activities.

  • Builds confidence discussing disability and inclusive design
  • Encourages teams to think differently about their audiences
  • Establishes understanding across user, employee, and client touch points
  • Equips teams to spot and fix common accessibility issues at all stages of the product life cycle

Manual Accessibility Testing

Format: 1–2 hours, half day, or deep dive
Who it’s for: Individuals or teams, from beginner to advanced

What it covers:
Learn how to start testing digital content using real assistive technologies: screen readers, keyboard-only navigation, magnifiers, switch devices, and voice control. Sessions are tailored to your team’s role, tools, and confidence level.

  • Demonstrates the importance of robust, flexible design to support real-world assistive technology use
  • Helps teams identify barriers early in the design or development process
  • Builds confidence using a range of devices and accessibility tools
  • Encourages shift-left testing and continuous improvement throughout the product lifecycle

Inclusive User Testing

Format: 1 day (or 2 half days)
Who it’s for: UX teams, product managers, researchers, consultants

What it covers:
How to plan and run inclusive testing with disabled participants. Covers planning, recruitment, facilitation, task design, and how to turn qualitative feedback into actionable insights.

  • Builds confidence to independently plan, facilitate, and report on inclusive user research
  • Helps teams design ethical, respectful sessions that meet participant needs
  • Strengthens skills in turning feedback into practical design and product decisions
  • Supports the development of scalable, inclusive research practices across teams

Auditing & Reporting for Accessibility

Format: Half or full day
Who it’s for: Developers, QA teams, accessibility juniors, consultants

What it covers:
A practical, hands-on session exploring how to scope, conduct, and report on accessibility audits. Includes selecting appropriate tools, identifying issues, mapping to WCAG, logging findings clearly, prioritising blockers, and integrating results into delivery workflows.

  • Builds confidence to carry out structured accessibility audits from start to finish
  • Equips participants to spot real user barriers – not just code-level errors
  • Develops practical skills in documenting, communicating, and prioritising accessibility findings
  • Helps teams embed accessibility into delivery processes in a collaborative way
  • Enables better conversations with stakeholders by connecting technical findings to user impact

Screen Reader Workshop (with Adi Latif)

Format: Half day
Who it’s for: Product teams, designers, engineers, QA testers, content creators

What it covers:
Led by blind accessibility expert Adi Latif, this session explores how screen reader users experience websites and apps — and where common pitfalls occur. Includes live demonstrations, breakout discussions, and practical takeaways.

  • Offers direct insight into assistive technology in action
  • Builds empathy and understanding across teams
  • Highlights barriers that automated tests often miss
  • Sparks ideas for user-led improvements
